# Publication Ethics

> **Exploratory prototype documentation**

## Principles

1. Publish only public-safe, non-confidential material.
2. Distinguish clearly between observation, hypothesis, and validation.
3. Keep AI-assisted outputs supervised, reviewed, and traceable.
4. Avoid misleading compatibility, endorsement, or performance claims.
5. Exclude branding assets unless permission is documented.
6. Preserve a strict boundary between private evidence and public-safe derivatives.
7. Keep public-facing language exploratory and non-commercial during the current project stage.

## Required labels for early-stage artifacts

Use one of the following labels when appropriate:

- `Exploratory`
- `Template`
- `Draft`
- `Unvalidated`

## Before public release

- remove sensitive metadata
- review images for labels, badges, serial numbers, or institutional identifiers
- verify that claims are evidence-backed
- confirm that cited references are public-safe
- confirm that raw source files, hidden metadata, and private measurements remain excluded

## Authorship and AI-assisted work

Engineering supervision, review, and final design decisions remain the responsibility of Daniel Ramírez Martínez. AI-assisted outputs may support the workflow, but they must remain supervised, reviewed, curated, versioned, and documented.
